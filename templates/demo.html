Project Demonstration

What We're Building

This demonstration shows our machine learning model that analyzes news articles to detect fake content. While trained on general news, our next model works especially well with politcal misinformation.

How It Works

We fine-tuned a BERT model to classify articles by analyzing their text. The model looks for patterns in word choice and sentence structure that often appear in misleading content.

Key Results

  • 99.2% accuracy on our test data
  • Precision: 99.1% | Recall: 99.3%
  • F1 Score: 0.991

Technical Details

Model Architecture

  • BertForSequenceClassification
  • bert-base-cased
  • Max sequence length: 512 tokens

Training Setup

  • 3 training epochs
  • Batch size: 8
  • Learning rate: 2e-5

Our Data

Data Sources

We used the Fake News Detection Datasets by Emine Bozkus:

  • False.csv: 10,700 fake articles
  • True.csv: 10,600 real articles

How We Prepared the Data

  • Combined and shuffled both datasets
  • Used only the article text for training
  • 80/20 train-test split
  • BERT tokenizer handled:
    • Lowercasing
    • Word splitting
    • Padding/truncation

What We Discovered

Word Patterns Tell the Story

Real News

Features formal language about:
"government", "policy", "report"

Fake News

Uses emotional language like:
"Shocking", "Breaking", "Exposed"
